monument_desc Hillcrest, located on the northern side of Bell Hill, Stapleton. The building is a two-storey rendered house with a steeply-pitched clay pantile roof and a stair tower at the centre of the rear, north-west facing, elevation. It is constructed of Pennant sandstone rubble with a rendered finish and appears to date to at least the seventeenth century.
Works were undertaken to the main, south-east facing, elevation to Bell Hill in the eighteenth century. This elevation is now of three bays with the main entrance door sited at the west end of the elevation. Above the door opening is a flat canopy supported on scrolled brackets the opening itself contains a four-panel door with an overlight. The fenestration consists of six-over-six pane sash windows.
A further phase of alteration was undertaken in the mid-1990s when internal alterations were made and the first-floor windows were replaced. Structural repairs were made to the roof in 1999 and the attic was altered at the same time.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/33/1942).
